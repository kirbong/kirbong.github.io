About

Welcome! My name is Kristen Marcinek

I am a graduate of Rutgers University - New Brunswick with a B.A. in Information Technology and Informatics. Currently, I am a Game Design MA candidate at American University, as well as a Museum Experience Associate with the National Children's Museum. I am also the former VP and a founding member of Rutgers Women's Gaming League.

I am interested in play, especially learning through play, and game design. Here you will see my portfolio surrounding these topics.

My technical skillset includes Unity Engine, C#, JavaScript, p5.js, other webdev tools like HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and PHP, game engines like Construct 3 and Stencyl, Python, and renpy.

I am also skilled in 2D illustration, 3D modeling, graphic design, Adobe Creative Cloud, verbal and written communication, and informal education (i.e. learning through play).

Thank you for stopping by!

Games

PixelFIGHT TCG (2022 - Current)

Pixel FIGHT! PixelFIGHT is a 2-player PvP sandbox trading card game inspired by similar games like Yu-Gi-Oh, Magic the Gathering, Minecraft, and Terraria. In PixelFIGHT, players can craft the strongest structures and the hardiest tools. The player who destroys all their opponent’s Structures and depletes their HP to 0 reigns victorious!

PixelFIGHT was created as the midterm for a Game Prototyping course taken in Fall of 2022. The main mechanic of the game is to build structures and destroy your opponent's field.

Trash Thrash (2021 - Current)

Welcome to Trash Thrash!

You are a lovely suburbanite just trying to get by, but your pesty neighbors aren’t letting that be possible. They’re loud, messy, and worst of all, TRASHY.

You’ve come up with a BRILLIANT plan; recruit neighborhood animals to destroy their reputation before they can destroy yours!

Fight to the figurative death with all your favorite trashy but cute animals.

Trash Thrash is a dicebuilding tabletop game centered around PVP combat. Players play as neighbors who deploy trash animals such as raccoons, mice, possums, and rats to destroy the other’s home. This game was developed as a semester-long final project for the Rutgers ITI Game Design Methodology course.

Holidays in Harkmall (2022)

Holidays in Harkmall was the first game I made in Ren'Py, a game engine that utilizes an alternate Python scripting language to create visual novels.

You are Rowan, a junior reporter working at Buzzread. After your most recent listicle flopping, you're on the hunt for the next big Christmas hit, and it just so happens to land you in your hometown of Harkmall, Massachusetts, aka everyone's mental image of a Christmas village. From "there's only one bed" to getting into sexy snowball fights, Harkmall is rife with Christmas cliches that'll make you think, "hey, is this an affectionate parody of a certain franchise of Christmas movies?"

My role in the game jam was as the programmer.
